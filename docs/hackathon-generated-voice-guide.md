# Hackathon Generated Voice Guide

## Goal

Use a generated voice as a fallback if you do not want to record your own narration.

The voice should feel:

- warm
- calm
- low-energy but confident
- slightly rough
- understated
- not polished
- not corporate

Do not aim for a direct imitation of any real actor or character.

## Recommended Voice Direction

Use a male voice with this overall character:

- relaxed and conversational
- mellow and easygoing
- slightly sleepy but still clear
- dry, understated tone
- natural American delivery
- slow to medium pace

## Voice Prompt

Use this prompt in your TTS tool:

```text
Male voice, relaxed and conversational, warm, low-energy but confident, slightly rough texture, mellow and easygoing, dry understated humor, natural American tone, slow-medium pace, human and unpolished, not announcer-like, not too dramatic, calm and casually intelligent.
```

## Alternative Voice Prompt

If the first voice sounds too polished, try:

```text
Male voice, very laid-back and mellow, warm and slightly sleepy, gently raspy, calm and effortless, casual American delivery, unhurried pace, understated humor, sounds relaxed, thoughtful, and completely unbothered.
```

## Delivery Rules

- avoid hype
- avoid trailer voice
- avoid startup promo energy
- leave natural pauses between thoughts
- let the voice sound a little loose instead of over-rehearsed

## Best Workflow

Do not render the full narration in one pass.

Split it into 4 blocks:

1. opening motivation
2. RuleShield explanation
3. shadow mode and feedback loop
4. future direction and closing

This makes it easier to:

- replace a weak block
- adjust pacing
- edit around awkward TTS phrasing
- keep the final result more natural
